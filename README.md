# Short Drama Dialogue Templates

Short drama dialogue needs to move fast: it must reveal conflict, expose secrets, define relationships, and create a reason to watch the next episode. This repository collects reusable dialogue templates for vertical short drama, micro-drama, AI film, and script-to-video workflows.

It is built for writers and AI production teams who need dialogue that can move directly into scripts, storyboards, and video generation.

## Why dialogue matters

In short drama, dialogue often carries more story work than traditional scenes:

- It introduces conflict quickly.
- It explains relationship tension.
- It reveals secrets.
- It creates emotional turns.
- It sets up cliffhangers.

Weak dialogue makes the episode feel flat, even if the premise is strong.

## Dialogue patterns

| Pattern | Use when | Example frame |
|---|---|---|
| Accusation | Start conflict immediately | "You knew the truth and still lied." |
| Refusal | Show power or emotional boundary | "I will not sign that." |
| Reveal | Change the audience's understanding | "That child is not his." |
| Reversal | Flip status in one line | "You are not firing me. I bought the company." |
| Cliffhanger | End with an open loop | "Then tell me why your name is on this file." |

## Scene structure

1. Start with tension, not greetings.
2. Give each character a goal.
3. Let dialogue reveal power changes.
4. Use short lines for mobile-first pacing.
5. End with a question, threat, reveal, or decision.
